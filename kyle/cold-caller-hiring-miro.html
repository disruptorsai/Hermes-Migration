Section 01
Why Hiring Is Non-Negotiable
Founder Leverage
Core Premise

Founder attention moves from doing the task to training the task.

Stop being in the weeds 2 hrs/day on cold calls. Redirect attention to better client results, close-rate improvements, and the next bottleneck.

Volume → Faster Feedback

You alone: 100–150 dials/day → script tests take weeks.

Team of 3+: 600 dials/day → run a 1,000-call test, iterate, run another. The feedback loop on script changes is way quicker.

Skill Transfer Philosophy

You can only transfer what you actually have. If you're at 1% ABR, three callers will sit at ~1%. If you're at 5%, you'll pull them up to 5%.

Action Step Before Hiring

If you're not doing 150–200 dials/day right now: do 1,000 calls yourself first (~8–9 days). Then you'll know 95% of what they'll ask — power dialer, GHL setup, niche objections, best call times — and can actually train them.

Section 02
Hiring Philosophy
Comp · Sources · Paths
Front-End Reality

The more front-end the role, the higher the churn.

Cold callers face rejection 2–3 hrs/day. VAs running ads on the back end don't. Design hiring assuming front-end churn is permanent — comp, training, and culture all flow from this.

❌ Don't — Per-Hour Pay

Lacks incentive. Callers pad hours, ignore appointment quality, and just want calendar fill to justify the timesheet.

✅ My Comp Structure

$25 / Qualified Show + 5–10% Commission

Pay-per-qualified-show forces them to book real fits. Commission on closes aligns them with the agency's outcome — and only fires if you can actually close.

Hiring Sources — Ranked
  • Indeed.com — best quality, group interviews 5–10 in one Zoom
  • Your network — agency-owner referrals
  • Cold-call community owners — DM trainers; they're incentivized to place students
  • Upwork.com — easier, lower quality
  • Fiverr.com — last resort
Two Hiring Paths

Blank-slate — train harder, longer; cheaper rate.

Past skill set — picks up faster; costs more.

Either way, they will ask niche-specific questions you must already know the answer to.

Why Submetrics Matter

Bad pickup % → number flagged spam in GHL (rotate it) or wrong call time (test 2pm vs 5pm).

Bad pitch → resonation → opener/hook is weak; rewrite the first 30 seconds.

Bad resonation → booking → close mechanic / calendar friction.

Setup

One Google Sheet per caller. They log Dials / Pickups / Pitches / Resonations / Bookings into their daily row. Percentages and totals autofill at the top.

Niche-specific. Contracting ≠ gyms ≠ real estate ≠ landscaping. Test for your vertical.

Section 04
Cold Calling SOP
Training · Feedback · Culture
Inside the SOP Doc
  • Brief company overview + niche
  • Role description (incentive framing)
  • Offer & pricing — written, not verbal
  • Sales process walkthrough
  • Daily checklist (2–3 hrs of calls)
  • GHL CRM walkthrough video
  • Calendly + EOD report links
  • Pre-call video reminder template
  • Objection-handling SOP (5–10 most common)
1Call Recording Reviews

Caller-driven: they send 5/wk or 2/day for feedback on weak calls.

Owner-driven: pull recordings from GHL sub-account, review 1–2 hrs/wk, DM feedback.

2End-of-Day Report (Google Form)
  • Total Dials
  • # Appointments Set
  • Self-rated performance 1–10
  • Notes / blocking objections

Forces self-reflection. Surfaces niche objection trends ("everyone asked about price today").

3Weekly Zoom + WhatsApp Group
  • Morning motivational videos in chat — ramp up before the rejection block
  • Weekly company calls on Zoom — share what's working, train
  • Win-drop ritual: when a caller books, they manually type the win in chat. Not automation. Creates natural competition: "John just got 3 — I need to catch up."
⚠ Group-Chat Caveat

The chat connects callers to each other. If the agency is bleeding (no closes, low pay), they group-think and turn on you. Only safe when you're closing.

Reality Check

Real callers vary wildly: A / B / C / D players. Some 1% ABR, some 5%. Some $300/wk, some $100. These are averaged — your weekly numbers will swing.

Caller Workload
