Your Role · Appointment Setter

Get qualified prospects to book live — while still on the phone.

✓ What You DO
  • Make outbound calls to prospects in our ICP
  • Open, hook, and qualify using the script
  • Book qualified prospects directly onto the calendar while on the call
  • Only send the booking link when you can't close it live (last resort)
  • Log every interested lead in GoHighLevel with status & notes
  • Send the prospect a confirmation text and reminder sequence
  • Submit your daily end-of-day report
✗ What You DO NOT Do
  • Quote pricing on the call (price is handled later)
  • Promise specific results or outcomes
  • Negotiate or discount
  • Book unqualified prospects just to hit a number
  • Default to sending a booking link instead of closing live
  • Skip CRM logging or end-of-day reports
Booking Live vs. Sending The Link

Always go for the live booking first.

While the prospect is on the phone with you, pull up the calendar and offer two specific time slots. Confirm, lock it in, send the calendar invite. This is how you should operate 90% of the time.

"I have a Tuesday at 2pm or a Wednesday at 10am, which works better?"

Only fall back to the booking link when:

They genuinely cannot commit to a time
In a meeting, driving, in the field. Qualified and interested but their hands are full.
They ask for a link to check their calendar
If they ask, send it. But always try a live time slot first before offering.
If you find yourself sending the link more than once or twice a day,
something is off. Bring it up in your next 1-on-1.

How To Ask About Revenue

Awkward to ask. The most important qualifier.

If they're under $20K/month, the engagement won't work. Don't skip this question. Don't soften it so much it becomes unclear. Use this exact line.

"Just so I make sure this is actually a fit before we book the call, can I ask roughly what your business is doing per month in revenue right now?"

If they say under $20K/month:

"Got it, appreciate the honesty. Truthfully we work best with businesses a bit further along, so I don't want to waste your time. I'll keep your info and reach back out when it makes sense."
Be respectful. Be clear. Move on. A no-fit handled well becomes a referral later.
Daily Checklist

Run the same play every shift.

Consistency wins. The best callers are the most boringly disciplined ones. Hit each step every day.

Call Hours
When You Dial
  • Minimum 2-3 hours of active dialing per shift, 5 days per week
  • Best windows: 9-11 AM and 2-4 PM in the prospect's local time zone
  • Test different windows for your vertical and report what works best
Pre-Shift
Before You Start
  • Log into GoHighLevel and confirm your call list is loaded
  • Check your dialer is connected with the right caller ID
  • Have the script, objection library, and qualification criteria open
  • Have the calendar pulled up and ready to book live
During
On The Phone
  • Follow the script. Don't freelance until you've booked at least 5 appointments
  • Update each interested lead's status in GoHighLevel immediately
  • Do NOT log leads who didn't pick up — save the CRM for real pipeline
  • Add notes for any callback or booking with the key context
  • If they book, send confirmation text and trigger reminder sequence
Post-Shift
After You Hang Up
  • Submit the end-of-day report before logging off
  • Drop wins into the team WhatsApp group
  • Note any objections you couldn't handle — we add them to the library
End-Of-Day Report

Submit this every shift. No exceptions.

This is how we coach you, fix what's not working, and reward what is. Skipping it isn't optional — it's how you keep the role.
