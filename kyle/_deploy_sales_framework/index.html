Phase One

Opener

Earn permission. Set the frame. Establish you are the consultant in the room — not a salesperson.

01 · Build Rapport 02 · Set the Frame
Stage 01 · Opener

Build Rapport.

Establish a professional, cordial connection quickly. Be direct. Earn permission to ask questions before diving deeper — you are not pitching yet.

Cold Call Opener
"Hey — I wanted to reach out because I genuinely believe I can help you save time and make money. My company is mastering the art of using AI to grow your presence online, get you more leads, and plug up inefficiencies. I do not want to assume what I do can help when I do not know your situation yet. Do you mind if we have a brief conversation just to see if there is something we can do to help you out?"
⚑ Hook: "Save time and make money" — that two-part promise is the whole opener. Keep it under 60 seconds.
Stage 02 · Opener

Set the Frame.

Position yourself as a consultant and trusted advisor — NOT a salesperson. Establish the agenda and get a verbal yes before discovery begins.

Frame Setting Script
"My job today is to figure out with you how AI can help you save time and make money. Our whole business model is helping people like you do 10x more without increasing cost or time. We start by understanding what is working and what is not. From there I can tell you where AI brings the best ROI. And later, if I determine we are actually a good fit, I will share exactly how we can help. Does that sound like a good thing to go through?"
Phase Two

Discovery

Map their reality, surface the dream, and quantify the pain. Listen more than you talk.

03 · Situation 04 · Desires 05 · Pain
Stage 03 · Discovery

Current Situation.

Understand where they are right now. Their tech, their wins, their bottlenecks — and the three numbers that drive every ROI calculation later.

Number 01
LTV
Lifetime value of a typical customer — the single most important number in the entire framework.
Number 02
CPA
What it currently costs them to acquire one customer through their existing channels.
Number 03
Monthly Revenue
Their current ballpark — the baseline against which every gap and every ROI is framed.
  • "What strategies have you used to get more clients? What worked, what did not?"
  • "What does your tech stack look like? Is your CRM dialed in?"
  • "What bottlenecks do you have, and where could AI help?"
Stage 04 · Discovery

Explore the Dream Outcome.

Find out exactly what success looks like to them. The wider the gap between their current state and their dream state, the more valuable your help becomes.

  • "What would you like to see happen in the next two to five years?"
  • "How many clients would your acquisition systems be bringing in?"
  • "How do you plan on removing yourself from day-to-day operations?"
  • "Tell me more about that — why is that goal important to you?"
  • "Based on your numbers, how many new clients would you need to hit that?"
⚑ Make the gap concrete. Use their LTV × target client count to put a real dollar figure on the dream. The dream becomes urgent the moment it has a number attached.
Stage 05 · Discovery

Pinpoint the Problem.

Surface the bottlenecks blocking them from the dream. Quantify the cost in real dollars and real hours. Nothing moves in a sale without real, felt pain.

  • "Given that goal — what do you feel are the biggest roadblocks right now?"
  • "How much is this lack of lead flow costing you per month?"
  • "How much revenue are you losing because of follow-up gaps — if you had to estimate?"
  • "Walk me through what happens after a lead comes in. Where does it fall through the cracks?"
  • "What is the thing that keeps you up at night about the business right now?"
⚑ Pain must be felt, not just acknowledged. Push them to put a real number on it. The more specific the pain, the stronger your ROI story will be later.
Phase Three

Assessment

Understand what they have tried, why it failed, and decide honestly whether you are the right fit.

06 · Past Solutions 07 · Qualification
Stage 06 · Assessment

Exhaust Previous Solutions.

Learn what they have tried, why it failed, and uncover their buying value — the criteria a solution must meet for them to commit.

Definition · Buying Value
A buying value is something the prospect feels they absolutely need before they will move forward. Discovered by learning what failed and what is still missing. Example: "I need to own everything we build — no vendor lock-in." That tells you to lead hard on IP ownership.
  • "What have you tried in the past to address [pain] — and what made it fail?"
  • "If you are comparing strategies, what would be the top one or two criteria that would make something genuinely worth implementing?"
Stage 07 · Assessment

Qualification — Go or No-Go.

Make a genuine, honest assessment of fit. Either invite them into the pitch with real conviction, or disqualify them respectfully and point them toward a better path.

If Qualified
"After everything we covered today, I genuinely believe what we do at Disruptors Media can be a massive game changer for your business. Since I think we are a strong fit, I would love to walk you through what I would suggest. Would you be open to that?"
If Not Qualified
"Based on what you've shared, I will be straight with you — I do not think we are the right fit at this stage." Then give them concrete direction. Disqualified prospects often send referrals.
Phase Four

Pitch

Show them what makes you different, prove it with transformation, then let them tell you the value.

08 · Differentiators 09 · Case Studies 10 · Trial Close
Stage 08 · Pitch

The Disruptors Difference.

Lead with the differentiators that map directly to their buying value and what burned them before. Use their own words as the contrast.

  • Fractional Chief AI Officer service — not a typical agency.
  • We build inside your software stack — no artificial dependencies, ever.
  • You own 100% of the IP. Remove us anytime and keep what we built.
  • We work live, side by side — unlike agencies that go silent after kickoff.
  • You direct our skill set wherever your ROI is highest.
  • Test multiple mechanisms — build one system at a time, highest ROI first.
⚑ Never ask "does that make sense?" Use open check-ins: "What thoughts are coming up?" / "What questions do you have right now?"
Stage 09 · Pitch

The Transformation Story.

Tell the story of a client who had a similar situation and achieved their dream outcome. Transformation is the word — not features, not services. Real before-and-after.

Step 01 · Set the Scene
"We worked with a [similar business] that was struggling with [their exact pain point]..."
Step 02 · What We Did
"What we did was [specific approach] — and because we built it inside their own stack, they kept everything..."
Step 03 · The Result
"Within [timeframe] they went from [specific before] to [specific after]..."
Step 04 · Connect Back
"That is actually really similar to what you described earlier when you mentioned [their pain point]..."
Stage 10 · Pitch

The Trial Close.

Have them articulate the value themselves. Self-articulated value is far more powerful than being told. Get them to a 10 before any dollar figure is mentioned.

Soft Close · The 1-to-10 Scale
"This might be a silly question — but on a scale of 1 to 10, where 1 means I just want to get off this call, and 10 means finances completely aside, this is something I could absolutely see myself doing — where do you fall right now?"
If Under 10 · Step 1
"Why not a 1? What are the things you are actually finding genuinely valuable about this?" — anchor on positives first.
If Under 10 · Step 2
"What is keeping it from being a 10? What do you need to see for this to feel like a clear yes — finances aside?"
Phase Five

Close

Frame the price as math. Run the loop on every objection. Then ask for the commitment cleanly — and stop talking.

11 · Pricing 12 · Objections 13 · Close
Stage 11 · Close

Pricing as ROI Math.

Never present price as a standalone number. By the time you state the fee, it should feel like a no-brainer they have already talked themselves into.

Leads Agent ROI
"If our leads agent delivers X leads per month, your team closes Y%, with an LTV of Z — even at half performance, that is [$X]. Does that more than cover our fee?"
Time Savings ROI
"What is your effective hourly rate? If we save you 10 hours a week, that is [rate × 10] per week — or [× 52] per year — back in your pocket. Does that alone pay for this?"
Combined ROI
"Between time savings and expected lead ROI, we are looking at [combined figure]. Our fee is [price]. That is a [X:1] return in the conservative scenario. Does this feel like a straightforward investment?"
⚑ After you say the price — silence. The next person to speak tends to lose their footing.
Stage 12 · Close

The Objection Loop.

Resolve concerns one by one with a structured, repeatable seven-step loop. Each pass gets you closer to the close. Commit this to memory.

01
Hear
02
Acknowledge
03
Get Clarity
04
Tie-Down
05
Resolve
06
Ace
07
Close Again
Step 04 · Tie-Down · The Most Critical Step
"I appreciate you sharing that. Setting this concern aside for a second — do you feel like what we are offering would genuinely get you to where you want to go? Why or why not?"
Stage 13 · Close

Ask for the Commitment.

Use a direct close question. Do not hedge. Do not soften. Then stop talking and wait — confidence carries the moment.

Direct Close
"When would you like to move forward?"
Agreement-First
"I will get the agreement sent over so you can review the IP ownership clause yourself. Under what name should I address it?"
Date-Setting
"Let us lock in a start date — does [X date] work to kick things off?"
⚑ After your close question — silence. Once committed, immediately confirm next steps: agreement, onboarding call, kickoff date.

Run the Play.
Close the Deal.
